# .impeccable.md — SocialPro Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Gaming and esports creators (streamers, content creators, competitive players) evaluating SocialPro as their talent management agency. Core market focus remains Spain, LatAm, and Türkiye. They are assessing trustworthiness, ambition, and prestige — "Can these people take my career seriously and level it up?"

**Secondary:** Brand marketers and media buyers looking to run influencer campaigns with gaming talent. They need confidence, operational clarity, and proof of results.

**Emotional job to be done:** A creator should feel "this agency is a step above — professional, credible, and deeply native to the gaming world." A brand should feel "these people can execute serious campaigns without chaos."

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Premium · Sharp · Credible

**Voice:** Authoritative without arrogance. Direct, not flashy. Results-first. Spanish-speaking, business-minded, internationally ambitious.

**Emotional goals:**
- Creators: ambition, trust, belonging
- Brands: confidence, proof, partnership
- Overall: never feel like a fan site, tournament microsite, or gaming blog

**Anti-references:** No generic esports-site tropes, no noisy neon-on-black by default, no streamer merch-shop energy, no over-rendered 3D gimmicks, no novelty fonts.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary visual system:** Dark, editorial, premium — closer to a high-end sports agency or design-forward management firm than a typical gaming brand. The orange → pink → purple gradient is the main brand signature and should be used with restraint.

**Secondary visual system:** Creator / giveaway surfaces may intentionally introduce a more cyber, neon-lime, high-energy look. This is a deliberate sub-brand expression, not drift. It should feel more experimental and creator-native, but still sit inside the SocialPro system through disciplined typography, spacing, hierarchy, and interaction quality.

**Color palette:**
- Primary brand: `#f5632a` → `#e03070` → `#c42880` → `#8b3aad` → `#5b9bd5`
- Core backgrounds: `#0e0e0e`, `#f5f4f1`, `#eceae6`
- Core text: `#0e0e0e`, `#6b6864`, `#a8a39d`
- Secondary creator/giveaway accent: neon-lime may appear as a controlled accent for specific sub-brand contexts

**Typography:**
- Display/headings: Barlow Condensed, weight 800–900, uppercase, tight tracking, compressed line-height
- Body/UI: Inter, weights 400–700
- Typography should carry most of the authority and energy

**Theme:** Dark-first foundation with intentional light-mode sections and full light/dark support where implemented. Dark carries ambition and prestige; light carries clarity and warmth.

**Motion:** Purposeful and restrained in the core brand. More expressive motion is allowed in creator/giveaway contexts, but it must still communicate state, focus attention, or reinforce delight — never become visual noise.

**References:** No specific products or sites to emulate. SocialPro should establish its own visual standard.

### Design Principles

1. **Credibility over hype.** Default to trust, clarity, and polish before spectacle.
2. **Creators are the centerpiece.** Talent identity, stats, and proof should stay visually foregrounded.
3. **One brand, two modes.** Main marketing surfaces are premium-editorial; creator/giveaway surfaces can be cyber-energetic. The difference is intentional, but the system must still feel cohesive.
4. **Typography leads.** Use Barlow Condensed for impact and Inter for clarity before reaching for decorative effects.
5. **Accessibility is baseline quality.** Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, reduced-motion respect, and avoid color-only meaning.
