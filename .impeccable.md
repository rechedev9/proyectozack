# .impeccable.md — SocialPro Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Gaming and esports creators (streamers, content creators, competitive players) evaluating SocialPro as their talent management agency. They are evaluating trustworthiness and prestige — "Can these people take my career seriously and level it up?"

**Secondary:** Brand marketers and media buyers looking to run influencer campaigns. They need confidence and proof of results.

**Emotional job to be done:** A creator visits and feels "this agency is a step above — professional, credible, and actually understands the gaming world." They self-select to apply.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Premium · Sharp · Credible

**Voice:** Authoritative without arrogance. Direct, not flashy. The agency that lets results speak. Speaks Spanish to a Spanish/Latin market but has international ambition.

**Emotional goals:**
- Creators: Ambition, trust, belonging ("this is where serious careers are made")
- Brands: Confidence, proof, partnership
- Should never feel like a fan site or a gaming blog — it's a business

**Anti-references:** Nothing that looks like a tournament overlay, streamer merch shop, or generic gaming site. No neon-on-black gamer aesthetic. No Comic Sans / Rajdhani / over-rendered 3D effects.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Dark, editorial, and premium — closer to a high-end sports agency than a gaming brand. The gradient (orange → pink → purple) is the signature energy; use it with restraint.

**Color palette:**
- `#f5632a` sp-orange → `#e03070` sp-pink → `#c42880` sp-dpink → `#8b3aad` sp-purple → `#5b9bd5` sp-blue
- Gradient is `135deg` for fills, `90deg` for text clips
- Backgrounds: `#0e0e0e` (near-black hero), `#f5f4f1` (off-white sections), `#eceae6` (bg2 alternates)
- Text: `#0e0e0e` (near-black), `#6b6864` (muted), `#a8a39d` (subdued)

**Typography:**
- Display/headings: Barlow Condensed, weight 800–900, uppercase, tight tracking (−0.5px to −1px), line-height ~0.88–0.95
- Body/UI: Inter, weights 400–700, letter-spacing 0 to 3.5px for labels
- Section tags: Inter 11px, weight 600, tracking 3px, uppercase — in gradient or brand pink

**Theme:** Both light and dark modes with a toggle. Dark-first (dark hero, light interior) is the base; full toggle support is required for accessibility and audience breadth.

**Motion:** Purposeful and restrained. Entrance animations (fadeUp, ~1s, staggered 0.2–0.5s delays). Hover: translateY(−3px to −5px) lifts, subtle box-shadow. Marquees for brand/creator scrolls. No gratuitous motion.

**Layout:**
- CSS Grid with `auto-fill / minmax` — fluid, no explicit breakpoints
- `clamp()` for fluid type scale
- 5vw horizontal padding throughout
- Section rhythm alternates dark ↔ light backgrounds

**References:** No close references — SocialPro sets its own standard. Closest analogues would be elite sports management agencies crossed with a design-forward creative studio.

### Design Principles

1. **Credibility over hype.** Every design decision should reinforce trust. Prefer restraint over spectacle. The gradient is signature energy — not decoration. Use it where it earns its place.

2. **Creators are the product.** Talent cards, photos, and stats are the centerpiece. Layouts must foreground creator identity — name, platform, numbers — without visual clutter.

3. **Dark and light coexist with purpose.** Dark sections carry weight and ambition; light sections carry clarity and warmth. Alternating rhythm keeps pages feeling dynamic without chaos. Both modes must look intentional, not just inverted.

4. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Barlow Condensed at large scale IS the energy. Body copy in Inter IS the credibility. Don't reach for decorative elements when type can do the job.

5. **Motion earns attention.** Every animation must serve communication (entrance sequence, hover feedback, state change). If removing it makes no difference, remove it.
